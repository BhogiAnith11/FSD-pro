A Century of Watchmaking

Our vintage collection draws inspiration from the most important decades of horology history.

1920s
The Birth of the Wristwatch
WWI soldiers needed hands-free timekeeping — the wristwatch was born from necessity. Cushion cases, wire lugs, and enamel dials defined the decade.
1940s
Military Precision
WWII drove massive innovation. Military spec field watches, hack movements, and legible dials became the standard. Many iconic references were born during this era.
1960s
The Space Age & Dive Watch Boom
The space race and scuba diving craze produced legendary tool watches. Rotating bezels, helium escape valves, and chronographs flourished in this golden era.
1980s
The Quartz Crisis & Survival
Japanese quartz nearly killed Swiss watchmaking. The survivors who persisted — focusing on mechanical craftsmanship — built the foundation of luxury watchmaking today.
🟤
What is Watch Patina?
Patina is the natural aging of a watch's dial, hands, and case over decades of wear. Creamy "tropical" dials, gilt numerals that fade to warm brown, and bronze cases that darken with character — these are not flaws. They're the soul of a vintage watch.
